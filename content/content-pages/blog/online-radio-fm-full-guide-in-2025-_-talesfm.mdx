## What is Online Radio FM?

If you aren't a "tech person," don't worry. Let’s break this down so it makes perfect sense.

How Old Radio Worked (The "Line of Sight" Problem)

Traditional FM/AM radio works by sending radio waves through the air from a giant tower. Imagine throwing a stone into a pond—the ripples only go so far before they disappear.

1. **Limitation 1:** If you drive too far from the city (usually 40-50 km), the signal dies.
2. **Limitation 2:** If there is a big mountain or building in the way, you get static noise.
3. **Limitation 3:** You can only listen to what is broadcasting nearby.

## How Online Radio Works (The "Global Pipeline")

Online Radio FM (or Internet Radio) doesn't use radio waves. It uses Data Packets sent over the internet. Think of it like a pipeline that connects your phone directly to the studio, no matter where it is.

**Benefit 1:** Zero Distance Limits. You can be sitting in a cafe in Mumbai and listening to a station in London, New York, or Buenos Aires as clearly as if you were there.

**Benefit 2:** TalesFM uses advanced AAC+ compression technology. This ensures you get high-fidelity audio that sounds like 320kbps MP3 but consumes data like a standard 128kbps stream. Expect to use around 60MB-90MB per hour.

**Benefit 3:** Metadata. Have you ever liked a song on the radio but didn't know the name? Online radio displays the Artist, Song Title, and Album Art right on your screen.

In 2025, with global internet penetration reaching 67.9% ([DataReportal 2025](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2025-global-overview-report)), having a radio that depends on local towers is outdated. Online radio is the logical upgrade.
