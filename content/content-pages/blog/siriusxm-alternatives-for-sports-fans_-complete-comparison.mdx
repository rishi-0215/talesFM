### **2. TuneIn Premium: The Direct SiriusXM Replacement**

If you love the "radio" feel of SiriusXM's sports coverage, TuneIn Premium is an alternative.

* **What You Get:** Live audio play-by-play for virtually every NFL game, MLB game, and a vast array of college sports. It also offers global sports feeds for soccer, rugby, and more.
* **Pros:** The coverage is extensive and reliable. The app is a straightforward, audio-first experience.
* **Cons:** It requires a paid subscription for full sports access. The free tier has limited sports content and includes ads.
* **Verdict: **Choose TuneIn Premium if you need a direct, app-based replacement for live play-by-play across multiple major sports.

### **3. ESPN+ / The ESPN App: The Sports Media Powerhouse**

ESPN+ has become a behemoth in the streaming world, offering a mix of live video and exclusive audio content.

* **What You Get: **Live video streams of out-of-market NHL, MLB, and MLS games, every UFC Fight Night, thousands of college sporting events, and a deep bench of original shows and analysis (like The Stephen A. Smith Show and other podcasts).
* **Pros: **Unbeatable for video content and original analysis. The value is huge if you're a fan of the leagues it covers.
* **Cons:** Does not carry live NFL games. It's a separate service from the main ESPN channel.
* **Verdict:** Choose ESPN+ if you want live video streams and deep-dive analysis from the worldwide leader in sports.

### **4. League-Specific Apps: Go Straight to the Source**

For the ultimate fan of one league, going directly to the official source is often the best option.

* **NFL Audio Pass:** Provides live home and away radio broadcasts for every NFL game, plus exclusive NFL Radio talk shows.
* **MLB Audio:** Offers home and away radio broadcasts for every baseball game throughout the season.
* **NBA League Pass Audio:** Follow your favorite NBA teams with live audio broadcasts.
* **Pros:** Comprehensive, league-focused, and often more affordable than all-in-one services.
* **Cons:** You'll need a separate subscription for each league you follow.
* **Verdict:** Choose a league app if your passion is dedicated to a single sport and you want the most complete coverage possible.

### **5. Local Radio Station Apps: For the Hometown Fan**

Never underestimate the power of the local flagship station.

* **What You Get:** By downloading the app for your local sports radio station (e.g., 98.5 The Sports Hub in Boston, 710 ESPN in Seattle), you can often stream their live broadcast for free.
* **Pros:** It's free and hyper-local, perfect for following the analysis and talk about your hometown teams.
* **Cons:** Coverage is limited to one market. Streaming rights can be spotty for live games, and the apps are often ad-heavy.
* **Verdict:** Choose a local app if you only care about one market's teams and want the most local perspective available.
