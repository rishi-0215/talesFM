## 2. TuneIn

A long-standing radio giant that offers thousands of live stations globally. The free version includes ads.

Highlights

* Local and global stations
* News networks (CNN, MSNBC, Bloomberg—some require a premium)
* Sports coverage (limited on free tier)

## 3. iHeartRadio

A strong choice if you enjoy curated playlists, podcasts, and artist stations. Expect ads, but the interface is familiar and easy.

Highlights

* Plenty of music stations
* Local radio from major cities
* Good podcast selection

## 4. Radio Garden

A unique, visual way to explore radio—spin the globe, pick a point, and listen. The experience is immersive but less practical for daily use.

Highlights

* Explore stations by location
* Minimalist and fun
* Great for discovering new cultures

## 5. Pandora (Free Tier)

Pandora’s algorithm-driven listening still feels magical, but the free version comes with ads and limited skips.

Highlights

* Dynamic music recommendations
* Artist and genre stations
* Simple, familiar layout

## 6. MyTuner Radio

A clean radio directory with thousands of streams. It’s a straightforward option for people who prefer traditional layouts.

Highlights

* 50,000+ stations
* Works across multiple devices
* Mixed music, news, and sports.

## 7. Audacy

A strong option for US listeners who want local stations, sports talk, and radio shows.

Highlights

* Good for talk radio
* Coverage of local news stations
* Sports and entertainment channels

## 8. Jango

A lightweight music-only service that creates genre and artist-based stations with minimal friction.

Highlights

* Good playlists
* Minimal ads
* Easy to use

## 9. AccuRadio

Loved for its genre depth and customizability, though the interface feels dated.

Highlights

* 1,000+ curated channels
* Custom station rules
* Great niche genres

## 10. LiveOne

Once known as Slacker Radio, it blends radio-style listening with playlists and events.

Highlights

* Artist stations
* Pop/EDM heavy catalogue
* Occasional live events
