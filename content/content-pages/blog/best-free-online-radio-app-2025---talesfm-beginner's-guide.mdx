## What Is Online Radio? (And How It's Different From Traditional Radio)

If you've only ever used traditional AM/FM radio, online radio might sound complicated. It's not.

**Traditional radio** broadcasts to a specific geographic region through radio waves. You tune into a frequency (like 98.5 FM), and you get whatever that station is playing right then. 

You're limited by geography, and if you drive out of range, the signal drops. Plus, you get interrupted by ads roughly every 10 minutes.

[Online radio](https://talesfm.com/) (also called internet radio or streaming radio) works completely differently. Instead of radio waves, it uses your internet connection to stream live broadcasts from stations around the world. 

Want to listen to a jazz station from New Orleans while you're eating breakfast in Delhi? Done. Want a classical station from Vienna during your work commute? Also done.

The key differences:

| **Feature**            | **Traditional Radio**              | **Online Radio (TalesFM)**    |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| **Geographic Reach**   | Limited to local region            | Worldwide access              |
| **Station Selection**  | 50-100 local stations              | 50,000+ global stations       |
| **Ads**                | Multiple interruptions             | Completely ad-free            |
| **Availability**       | Limited to broadcast hours         | 24/7 streaming                |
| **Device Flexibility** | Car/home radio only                | Any internet-connected device |
| **Cost**               | Sometimes requires cable TV bundle | 100% free forever             |

This is why online radio has exploded in popularity. You're not just getting better access—you're getting actual freedom.
